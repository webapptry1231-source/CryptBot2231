In the backtest, each trade is treated independently, which inflates the trade count artificially. The "23 trades" are really closer to **9–10 independent market decisions.** This is a form of backtest inflation.

### Problem B: Same-direction stacking on identical asset
There is no deduplication logic. The bot scans BTC/USDT every 15 minutes and fires a signal if score ≥ 60. On a trending day (either direction), it will stack 4–8 simultaneous positions in the same direction. This is not diversification — it is leverage amplification without acknowledgement.

### Problem C: No market regime detection
All 23 trades are LONG. The bot has no short capability and no "market is trending down — stop generating longs" logic. On Mar 17–18, BTC dropped ~3% and the bot fired 14 consecutive long signals, all of which lost. A basic regime filter (e.g., "if price < EMA200 on 4h chart, do not take 15m longs") would have eliminated the entire Mar 17–18 loss cluster.

### Problem D: Score 65 inverted performance
As noted above: Score 65 = 0% win rate. This score tier is more common than Score 75 but actively destroys capital. The scoring algorithm is generating a "dead zone" at 65 that is statistically worse than not trading.

### Problem E: Backtester exit-time bug
Several trades show exit times before their entry times (same-day basis). This indicates the backtester is not correctly tracking individual trade exit times when multiple trades are open simultaneously. All exits on Mar 17 that show "11:30" appear to reference the same market level (BTC at 72782.0), suggesting the backtester found one common SL event and assigned it to all open trades — correct in outcome but the hold-time timestamps are wrong.
